The Problem

Users cannot track calories while ordering food. Tracking is manual, time-consuming, and inconsistent. When ordering out, users lose control of their diet goals entirely.

?

Current Reality

Users either don't track calories at all, use separate apps like MyFitnessPal, or avoid ordering food entirely just to maintain control over their diet.

Competitive Gap

What nobody
has built yet

📊

No Calorie Tracking

Current platforms lack built-in calorie tracking systems during the ordering process. The data is never shown where it matters most.

🗄️

Limited Data

Calorie information is not consistently available across restaurants — creating a fragmented, unreliable experience.

🔗

No Habit System

Absence of gamification, streaks, or progress tracking features means no sticky behavior loop for healthy eating.

📵

Disconnected Experience

Health and ordering remain separate, requiring users to switch apps — breaking the flow and killing compliance.

Why This Works

The case for building this

🔗

Unified Experience

Everything in one app eliminates friction and manual tracking effort. No context-switching, no drop-off.

📈

Market Timing

Rising health awareness and fitness app usage create strong demand. The behavioral shift is already happening.

Risks to Consider

R1

Inaccurate calorie data from restaurants — requires systematic data collection and partner onboarding

R2

User burnout from streak pressure — needs a forgiving mechanic like streak shields

R3

Limited restaurant calorie information — partial rollout may feel incomplete

R4

Data accuracy and verification challenges — nutritional claims must be trustworthy
